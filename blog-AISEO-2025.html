AI Overviews and Zero-Click Searches Are Changing the SERP

Google’s search results page is undergoing its most radical transformation in 25 years, shifting from a list of webpage links to AI-generated answers that fundamentally alter how people find information online.

What are AI Overviews and How They Work

AI Overviews are AI-generated summaries that appear at the top of Google search results, providing instant answers to user queries. Unlike traditional featured snippets that extract exact text from a webpage, AI Overviews use Google’s Gemini large language model to create comprehensive summaries that combine information from multiple sources.

These summaries are designed to give users a quick overview of a topic or answer complex questions directly on the search page. Currently, with informational intent and 19% with commercial intent, AI Overviews appear for 59% of searches. They’ve expanded dramatically, now available in over 200 countries and more than 40 languages, with Google reporting a 10% rise in usage for queries that feature them.

Behind the scenes, AI Overviews use a “query fan-out” technique that issues multiple related searches across subtopics and data sources to develop comprehensive responses. This allows Google to display a wider and more diverse set of helpful links than with classic web search.

Impact on CTRs and User Behaviour

  • Ahrefs found that when AI Overviews were present, position 1 CTR decreased by 34.5%.
  • Amsive reported an average 15.49% CTR drop, with much larger losses in specific cases.
  • Publishers are seeing per-query clickthrough rate losses of almost 50% on desktop and 37.7% on mobile.
  • Pew Research: Users who encountered an AI summary clicked on a traditional search result link in just 8% of visits, compared to 15% for those who didn’t see an AI summary. Users rarely clicked links within summaries—just 1% of visits.

Why Traditional Blue Links are Losing Visibility

AI Overviews consume premium top-of-page space, pushing organic listings down. On some queries, Google now shows fewer than ten traditional links on the first page. This contributes to the growing “zero-click” phenomenon, with about 60% of all Google searches not resulting in clicks.

Viewports of AI Overviews can be as high as 3,200 pixels, more than most screen resolutions. Consequently, top organic results are often “below the fold,” requiring significant scrolling. Additionally, AI search prioritizes entities, reputation, and authority signals alongside traditional SEO factors.

LLMs and Chatbots – The New Search Alternatives

AI-powered tools, like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini, are reshaping how users find information online. ChatGPT alone now refers around 10% of new Vercel signups, up from just 1% six months ago. These tools provide conversational, direct answers instead of lists of links.

Tips for SEO Strategising in 2025

  • Optimize for entities, not just keywords: LLMs favor structured, factually rich content that directly answers questions. Establish topical authority.
  • Build mentions across platforms: Frequent mentions across news, blogs, forums, and social media increase visibility in AI systems.
  • Prioritize conversion quality: Visitors from AI platforms are 4.4x more likely to convert than those from traditional search.

User-Generated Content (UGC) as a Ranking Factor

UGC now impacts SEO. Reddit and Quora traffic have grown significantly post-Google November 2023 update. Embedding customer photos, videos, and reviews provides social proof and improves engagement metrics like dwell time and bounce rate.

Conclusion

The SEO landscape is changing: AI Overviews dominate search results, LLMs offer alternatives, and UGC directly influences rankings. Brands must adapt by combining AI tools with human oversight, focusing on real user problems, and establishing authority. Delivering exceptional value remains the key to success in 2025 and beyond.
